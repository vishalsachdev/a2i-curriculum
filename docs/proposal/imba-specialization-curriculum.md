# iMBA Specialization in Applied AI: Proposed Curriculum

> **Document Purpose:** This document outlines a proposed curriculum for an Applied AI specialization within the existing iMBA program at Gies College of Business. This specialization would consist of 4-5 courses (12-15 credits) that can be integrated into the iMBA program structure.

---

## Overview

**Specialization Name:** Applied AI for Business Leadership  
**Total Credits:** 12-15 credits (4-5 courses)  
**Format:** Fully online via Coursera (existing iMBA platform)  
**Duration:** Each course follows iMBA's existing course structure (typically 4-6 weeks)  
**Prerequisites:** Completion of iMBA core courses (or concurrent enrollment)  
**Target Audience:** iMBA students seeking to specialize in AI leadership and transformation

---

## Curriculum Structure: Two Options

### Option 1: Light Specialization (4 courses, 12 credits)

**Recommended for initial launch** - Provides essential AI business leadership skills while maintaining flexibility for students.

### Option 2: Deep Specialization (5 courses, 15 credits)

**Recommended for expansion** - Offers more comprehensive coverage of AI topics, including hands-on technical work.

---

## Option 1: Light Specialization (4 Courses, 12 Credits)

### Course 1: AI Strategy & Business Framing (3 credits)
**Course Code:** iMBA-AI-601  
**Duration:** 4-6 weeks (aligned with iMBA course format)  
**Prerequisites:** iMBA core courses (Strategy, Operations, or concurrent)

#### Learning Objectives
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
- Position AI initiatives within organizational missions and stakeholder needs
- Diagnose coherence gaps across data, workflows, and governance
- Evaluate AI vendors and make build-vs-buy decisions
- Develop business cases for AI initiatives with ROI analysis
- Assess technology readiness and organizational capability

#### Course Content
**Week 1-2: Strategic AI Positioning**
- AI in business context: opportunities and challenges
- Stakeholder analysis for AI initiatives
- Portfolio prioritization frameworks
- Business Coherence Scorecard introduction

**Week 3-4: Vendor Evaluation & Build vs. Buy**
- AI vendor landscape and evaluation frameworks
- Build vs. buy decision models
- Technology readiness assessment
- Cost-benefit analysis for AI initiatives

**Week 5-6: Business Case Development**
- ROI analysis for AI projects
- Risk assessment and mitigation
- Resource planning and budgeting
- Executive presentation skills

#### Assessments
- **Strategic AI Initiative Proposal (40%)**: Develop a business case for an AI initiative in student's organization
- **Vendor Evaluation Framework (30%)**: Evaluate and compare AI vendors for a specific use case
- **Peer Review & Discussion (30%)**: Participate in peer feedback sessions and case discussions

#### Integration with Existing iMBA Content
- Builds on iMBA Strategy courses
- Connects to Operations and Innovation courses
- Uses existing iMBA case study methodology

---

### Course 2: Data & AI Infrastructure for Business (3 credits)
**Course Code:** iMBA-AI-602  
**Duration:** 4-6 weeks  
**Prerequisites:** iMBA-AI-601 (recommended) or concurrent

#### Learning Objectives
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
- Understand data architecture fundamentals for AI applications
- Design inclusive data architectures that support business objectives
- Evaluate data quality, observability, and lineage requirements
- Develop data monetization strategies
- Manage multi-vendor AI platform integrations

#### Course Content
**Week 1-2: Data Architecture Fundamentals**
- Data architecture patterns for AI (no coding required)
- Cloud platforms overview (GCP, AWS, Azure)
- Data quality and governance frameworks
- Privacy, security, and accessibility considerations

**Week 3-4: Data Strategy & Monetization**
- Data-as-asset frameworks
- Data monetization strategies
- Data marketplace dynamics
- Building data-driven business models

**Week 5-6: Platform Integration & Management**
- Multi-vendor AI platform management
- Integration strategies and best practices
- Vendor relationship management
- Platform selection criteria

#### Assessments
- **Data Architecture Design (40%)**: Design a data architecture for an AI use case
- **Data Monetization Strategy (30%)**: Develop a data monetization plan
- **Platform Integration Case Study (30%)**: Analyze and recommend platform integration approach

#### Integration with Existing iMBA Content
- Connects to Information Systems courses
- Builds on Operations and Supply Chain courses
- Uses existing iMBA project-based learning approach

---

### Course 3: AI Governance & Ethics (3 credits)
**Course Code:** iMBA-AI-603  
**Duration:** 4-6 weeks  
**Prerequisites:** iMBA-AI-601 (recommended)

#### Learning Objectives
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
- Navigate regulatory requirements for AI systems
- Develop accountability frameworks and audit trails
- Design governance structures for AI initiatives
- Address bias, fairness, and ethical considerations
- Implement compliance frameworks (COBIT, ISO, GDPR)

#### Course Content
**Week 1-2: Regulatory Landscape**
- AI regulation overview (EU AI Act, US state laws, sector-specific)
- Compliance requirements and frameworks
- Risk assessment and mitigation
- Legal considerations for AI deployment

**Week 3-4: Governance & Accountability**
- AI governance frameworks
- Accountability structures and audit trails
- Cross-functional governance models
- Board-level reporting and communication

**Week 5-6: Ethics & Fairness**
- Bias detection and mitigation
- Fairness frameworks and evaluation
- Ethical AI design principles
- Building trust in AI systems

#### Assessments
- **Governance Framework Design (40%)**: Develop an AI governance framework for an organization
- **Compliance Assessment (30%)**: Conduct a compliance audit for an AI use case
- **Ethical Analysis Case Study (30%)**: Analyze ethical implications and propose solutions

#### Integration with Existing iMBA Content
- Connects to Business Law and Ethics courses
- Builds on Corporate Governance courses
- Uses existing iMBA case study methodology

---

### Course 4: AI Transformation Capstone (3 credits)
**Course Code:** iMBA-AI-604  
**Duration:** 6-8 weeks (capstone project)  
**Prerequisites:** Completion of iMBA-AI-601, 602, 603

#### Learning Objectives
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
- Develop comprehensive AI transformation roadmaps
- Lead organizational change for AI adoption
- Measure transformation success and ROI
- Communicate AI strategy to executive leadership
- Build data-driven organizational cultures

#### Course Content
**Week 1-2: Transformation Strategy**
- AI transformation frameworks
- Change management for AI adoption
- Building organizational readiness
- Stakeholder engagement strategies

**Week 3-4: Implementation Planning**
- Phased rollout strategies
- Resource allocation and budgeting
- Risk management and mitigation
- Success metrics and KPIs

**Week 5-6: Leadership & Communication**
- Executive communication and board presentations
- Building AI literacy across organization
- Managing resistance and building trust
- Creating centers of excellence

**Week 7-8: Capstone Project**
- Develop comprehensive AI transformation plan
- Present to peer review panel
- Reflect on learning and application

#### Assessments
- **AI Transformation Roadmap (50%)**: Comprehensive transformation plan for student's organization
- **Executive Presentation (30%)**: Present transformation plan to peer panel
- **Reflection Portfolio (20%)**: Document learning journey and application

#### Integration with Existing iMBA Content
- Serves as capstone integrating all specialization courses
- Connects to Leadership and Change Management courses
- Uses existing iMBA capstone project format

- Platform economics and network effects
- AI pricing strategies and value capture
- Market structure and competitive dynamics
- Regulation and antitrust considerations

**Week 5-6: Business Model Innovation**
- AI-native business models
- Value proposition design for AI products
- Revenue model innovation
- Market sizing and opportunity assessment

#### Assessments
- **Pricing Model Analysis (40%)**: Develop pricing strategy for an AI product/service
- **Business Model Canvas (30%)**: Design AI-native business model

## Assessment Strategy

### Alignment with iMBA Assessment Philosophy

**iMBA Assessment Approach:**
- Mix of individual and team assignments
- Peer review and feedback
- Real-world application projects
- Discussion participation

**AI Specialization Enhancements:**
- **Executive Presentations**: Board-ready presentations (aligns with MBA focus)
- **Business Cases**: ROI analysis and financial modeling (business school emphasis)
- **Portfolio Documentation**: GitHub-based learning portfolios (modern, technical)
- **Peer Review**: 360-degree feedback on leadership and collaboration

### Assessment Breakdown (per course)

| Assessment Type | Weight | Description |
|----------------|--------|-------------|
| **Major Project** | 40-50% | Real-world application project (business case, framework design, etc.) |

## Comparison: Specialization vs. Full MS Degree

| Dimension | iMBA Specialization | Full MS Degree |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------|
| **Credits** | 12-15 credits | 36 credits |
| **AI Focus** | 17-21% of degree | 100% AI focus |
| **Duration** | 2-3 years (with MBA) | 18-24 months |
| **Tuition** | $22-25K (iMBA pricing) | $45-55K |
| **Target Market** | MBA-seeking students | AI-focused professionals |
| **Startup Investment** | $200-400K | $700K-$1M |
| **Time to Market** | 6-9 months | 12-18 months |

**Key Insight:** Both options can coexist, serving different market segments. The specialization serves MBA-seeking students, while the MS degree serves those who want deep AI focus without MBA.
