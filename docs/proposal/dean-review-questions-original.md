### 3. Faculty Resources & Opportunity Cost

**QUESTION:** Do we have faculty capacity for this, and what's the opportunity cost?

**Concerns:**
- Proposal requests 8-10 core faculty (0.5-0.75 FTE each)
- Business analytics and information systems faculty are already teaching iMBA, MS Management, undergrad
- IS department has limited faculty - this would consume significant capacity
- What courses in existing programs would be cut or taught by adjuncts?

**What needs to be addressed:**
- Specific faculty names and their current teaching loads
- Analysis of impact on existing programs (iMBA, MS Management, undergrad)
- Plan for backfilling courses that get displaced
- Evidence that we can hire qualified new faculty (tight market for AI + business faculty)

### 4. Why Not MBA Specialization Instead?

**QUESTION:** Why create separate MS degree rather than AI specialization within proven iMBA?

**Advantages of iMBA Specialization:**
- Leverage existing brand and student pipeline
- Lower risk (test market with specialization before committing to new degree)
- Share resources with existing iMBA infrastructure
- Cross-selling: students get MBA + AI specialization
- Faster time to market (no new degree approval process)

**Disadvantages of Separate MS:**
- New degree = new accreditation review, longer approval timeline
- Competes with our own iMBA
- Higher price point may limit market
- More faculty resources required
- Duplicates some existing iMBA content (strategy, analytics, etc.)

**What needs to be addressed:**
- Compelling argument why separate degree is better strategic choice
- Analysis showing iMBA specialization wouldn't serve market
- Explanation of how this grows overall Gies enrollment vs. shifts students between programs

### 5. Revenue Projections Seem Optimistic

**QUESTION:** Are enrollment projections realistic given competitive landscape and Gies positioning?

**Concerns:**
- Proposal assumes 30 ‚Üí 100 ‚Üí 150 students in Years 1-3
- Georgia Tech OMSCS: 10,000 students at $7K (why not them?)

### 6. Technical Prerequisites & Student Preparation

**QUESTION:** How do we ensure business students can handle technical content?

**Concerns:**
- Proposal says "no coding required" but courses involve:
  - Cloud platforms (GCP, BigQuery, Vertex AI)
  - Agent operations and evaluation
  - Data infrastructure and analytics engineering
- Target students are product managers, consultants - many lack technical background
- Risk of high attrition if students overwhelmed by technical demands

**What needs to be addressed:**
- Clear articulation of minimum technical prerequisites
- Pre-program bootcamp or bridge courses?
- Support systems for less technical students
- Evidence from peer programs on retention rates for non-technical students

### 7. Accreditation & AACSB Implications

**QUESTION:** Have you engaged with AACSB and regional accreditor about this program?

**AACSB Concerns:**
- Is this a "business" degree (AACSB) or "applied science" degree (different accreditation)?
- Faculty qualifications: Do we have enough SA/PA/AQ faculty for AI topics?
- Learning outcomes: Are these appropriate for business school MS degree?
- How does this fit within our existing AACSB continuous improvement plan?

**Process Concerns:**
- Substantive change notification required?
- Timeline: AACSB review could add 6-12 months
- Regional accreditor (HLC) also requires notification for new degree

**What needs to be addressed:**
- Confirmation that AACSB and HLC have been consulted (even informally)
- Faculty qualifications matrix showing SA/PA/AQ status
- Clear argument this is business degree, not applied CS degree
- Timeline accounting for accreditation reviews
