# A²I 643 – Innovation Leadership in Intelligent Systems

## Course Purpose

A²I 643 equips learners to translate mission-aligned insights into transformative initiatives—whether as corporate innovation leaders, venture founders, or social entrepreneurs. The course recognizes that not all business students want to launch startups, offering three distinct pathways that honor governance, evidence, and inclusion commitments while preparing leaders to orchestrate AI-driven change in diverse contexts.【F:docs/context/conversation.md†L116-L135】

## Three Innovation Pathways

### 1. Corporate Innovation Track (60% of students)
- Leading AI transformation within existing organizations
- Developing intrapreneurship capabilities and innovation labs
- Managing change, building data-driven cultures, and stakeholder alignment
- Building centers of excellence and scaling AI initiatives

### 2. Venture Creation Track (25% of students)
- Traditional startup path with product-market fit validation
- Alignment with iVenture Accelerator and Origin Ventures
- VC-ready pitch development and fundraising strategies
- Building AI-native business models

### 3. Social Innovation Track (15% of students)
- Public sector digital transformation initiatives
- NGO/nonprofit AI applications for mission delivery
- Social enterprise models and impact measurement
- Community-owned data cooperatives and governance

## Assessment Structure (Business School Aligned)

Assessments mirror real business evaluation contexts, preparing students for executive decision-making environments.

- **Business Case & Executive Proposal (30%)** – Comprehensive business case including financial analysis, ROI projections, risk assessment, and resource requirements. Evaluated on strategic alignment, financial rigor, and stakeholder impact.【F:docs/context/conversation.md†L82-L88】【F:docs/context/conversation.md†L167-L193】

- **Applied Innovation Portfolio (30%)** – GitHub repository documenting: architecture decisions, prototype development, governance framework, evidence validation, and iteration cycles. Demonstrates coherence between strategy and execution.【F:docs/context/conversation.md†L76-L109】【F:docs/context/conversation.md†L124-L135】

- **Executive Presentation & Viva (25%)** – 15-minute board-ready presentation followed by Q&A from faculty, industry mentors, and pathway-specific evaluators. Assessed on communication clarity, strategic thinking, and handling of challenging questions.【F:docs/context/conversation.md†L76-L120】【F:docs/context/conversation.md†L135-L177】

- **360 Peer & Self-Assessment (15%)** – Leadership effectiveness, collaboration quality, and contribution to peer learning. Cross-pathway feedback emphasizes adaptability and professional skills.【F:docs/context/conversation.md†L144-L152】【F:docs/context/conversation.md†L156-L163】

### Pathway-Specific Assessment Emphasis

**Corporate Innovation:** Change management strategy (40%), organizational readiness assessment (30%), executive communication (30%)

**Venture Creation:** Market validation evidence (40%), financial projections and unit economics (30%), investor pitch quality (30%)

**Social Innovation:** Community impact assessment (40%), stakeholder governance framework (30%), sustainability model (30%)
